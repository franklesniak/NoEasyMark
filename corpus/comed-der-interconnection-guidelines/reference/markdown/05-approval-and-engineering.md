# Application Approval and Interconnection Agreement

For interconnection request Levels 2, 3, 4, and for large DER facilities, conditional approval will be granted after the countersignature of the Interconnection Agreement. For Level 1 interconnection requests that do not require utility upgrades, the conditional approval will be granted after the completion of all technical studies. In cases where an upgrade is required for a Level 1 application, conditional approval will be granted after the countersignature of the Interconnection Agreement. For full operation of a facility, an Interconnection Customer must complete and comply with Part 466, Appendix B, Certificate of Completion.

## Protective Relay Requirements and Review

ComEd will review and approve the general design of the protection scheme for a proposed DER facility. Review of the customer protective scheme will include all ComEd required protective functions which directly affect protection of the ComEd system and all functions which, as determined by ComEd, must operate to minimize the impact of customer faults and abnormal conditions on the ComEd system. The Interconnection Customer is responsible for the facility protection design of customer owned facilities and equipment. ComEd review of customer system protection schemes and settings does NOT imply either implicitly or explicitly that this will provide adequate protection or performance of customer-owned facilities.

The Interconnection Customer must furnish, for ComEd's approval, relay function diagram, three-line diagrams, one-line diagram, relay product data sheets and proposed settings for relays specified on the Relay Functional Requirements Specifications (RFRS) form. If requested, ComEd, when required, will provide system data needed to determine the relay settings. See [Interconnecting Customer Engineering Design](08-design-requirements.md#interconnecting-customer-engineering-design), [Protection Requirements](09-protection-requirements.md) and [Appendix 4](16-appendix-4-relay-protection.md) for more information.

## Engineering, Construction, and Inspection

ComEd strongly recommends that the Interconnection Customer not procure, construct, or build any facilities until after the interconnection reviews are complete and the Interconnection Customer subsequently receives conditional approval or an Interconnection Agreement. The Interconnection Customer is responsible for all permitting and electrical inspections that are required through their Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ).

If interconnection facilities and/or distribution upgrades are needed to interconnect a proposed DER facility, a construction schedule will be mutually agreed-to and will be included as an appendix to the Interconnection Agreement. A ComEd project manager will then be assigned to manage the execution of ComEd engineering, procurement, and construction activities related to the project.

### Testing & Inspection

ComEd may, at its discretion, require a witness test of an installed DER facility before granting permission to operate the facility in parallel with the ComEd distribution system. Witness testing is required for any DER facility using equipment that is not lab-certified (ref. Part 466, Section 466.70), and is generally required for larger DER facilities, whether lab-certified or not. The Interconnection Customer is responsible for providing qualified testing personnel who will complete all ComEd required witness tests under the supervision of a ComEd designated testing engineer. In some cases, ComEd personnel may perform witness testing in coordination with the Interconnection Customer if the operation of ComEd owned equipment is required to conduct testing.

The Interconnection Customer is responsible for ensuring that all circuit breakers, controls, relays and other protective devices are adjusted and functioning correctly. The Interconnection Customer shall provide test equipment and qualified personnel to perform the required tests. ComEd will provide a list of specific required tests to be witnessed (also see [Appendix 2](14-appendix-2-inspection-standards.md)). The Interconnection Customer is responsible for providing ComEd with any additional detail on customer-owned protection equipment (if required). This includes, but is not limited to, customer inverter settings (customer inverter settings shall be provided on ComEd Pre Witness Testing Form along with an electronic copy of the inverters settings for each inverter in PDF format) (Electronic PDF copies of customer inverter settings shall clearly identify each inverter parameter which corresponds to a ComEd required setting), customer recloser settings, etc.

Testing & Inspection for DER Facility:

- ComEd may require witness testing before allowing parallel operation with its distribution system.
- Witness testing is mandatory for non-lab-certified equipment and generally for larger DER facilities, regardless of certification.
- The Interconnection Customer must provide qualified testing personnel supervised by a ComEd designated engineer.
- ComEd personnel may participate in witness testing if ComEd equipment is involved.

Interconnection Customer Responsibilities for Testing:

- Ensure proper functioning of circuit breakers, controls, and protective devices.
- Provide test equipment and qualified personnel for required tests.
- ComEd will provide a list of specific tests to be witnessed (refer to [Appendix 2](14-appendix-2-inspection-standards.md)).
- Interconnection Customer must share details of customer-owned protection equipment, including inverter and recloser settings, if necessary.

Pre-testing Requirements:

- Interconnection Customer must test and inspect its distributed generation facility and interconnection equipment before ComEd's witness testing and interconnection.
- Interconnection Customer must obtain written authorization from ComEd before operating its distributed generation facility in parallel with ComEd's electric distribution system.

The Interconnection Customer shall test and inspect its distributed generation facility, including the interconnection equipment prior to any ComEd required witness testing and prior to interconnection. The Interconnection Customer shall not operate its distributed generation facility in parallel with the ComEd's electric distribution system without prior written authorization by the ComEd.

## Customer Requested Outages

If a customer requires an outage to interconnect their solar facilities to ComEd's system, or to perform any work on their system, the customer needs to call 1-800-EDISON1 or their customer account manager and request an outage. Please note, if the interconnection requires that a customer transformer must be upgraded or replaced, the customer can coordinate its outage with the outage ComEd requires to perform its work. ComEd resources require at least 12 weeks notice prior to the desired start of any work. This applies to outage and non-outage work, as well as instances where ComEd resources need to be re-scheduled. Community Supply, Commercial, and Industrial customers shall work through their assigned ComEd Project Manager as soon as they become aware of the need to change their work schedule.

## Equipment Changes

After a complete DER interconnection application is submitted to ComEd, the Interconnection Customer is required to provide a Modification Request form through the interconnection portal for any changes to the originally proposed DER system. ComEd will review the modification to determine whether the change is deemed to be material pursuant to the process and criteria set forth in Part 466.125 Material Modification. ComEd will notify customer of the results of the modification request analysis via the interconnection portal.

## Certificate of Completion

Once ComEd determines that a DER facility may be operated in parallel with the ComEd distribution system, the Interconnection Customer will be required to submit photos of inverter nameplates, submit proof of electrical inspection from Authority Holding Jurisdiction (AHJ), and submit any as-built drawings to ComEd.

Once all documentation is received, both the Interconnection Customer and the ComEd representative will sign and date the "Approval for Operation of Generating Facility in Parallel with the ComEd System" document (Certificate of Completion). Once completed, the generation facility is granted permission to operate in parallel with the ComEd System.

> **Warning**: Livening equipment without the required permission to operate will result in disconnection from the ComEd system.
