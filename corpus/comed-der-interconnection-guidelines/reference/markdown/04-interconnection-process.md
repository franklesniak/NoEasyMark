### Pre-Application

Pursuant to the interconnection rules, prior to applying for interconnection, a potential Interconnection Customer may submit a request, along with a non-refundable \$300 fee, for a pre-application report on a proposed project at a specific site. ComEd will provide the preapplication data to the Interconnection Customer within 20 business days after receipt of the completed pre-application report request form and fee. For interconnection to the distribution system, the pre-application report will preliminarily identify up to two distribution circuits within one quarter mile of the proposed point of interconnection that are potentially available for interconnection. The report may include the following information:

- Substation and line/feeder identifying information and configuration
- Distance to three-phase electric lines, if applicable
- Existing and pending aggregate generation capacity interconnected to the identified substations and circuits
- Capacity of the substation transformer, substation circuit equipment, and lowest rated circuit equipment between the proposed point of interconnection and the substation/area bus identified
- Circuit peak and minimum load (estimated if actual is unavailable)
- Approximate circuit distance between the proposed point of interconnection and the identified substation or area bus
- Disclosure from ComEd as to whether the aggregate contingent upgrades of the systems earlier in the queue are estimated to exceed certain thresholds, if such estimates are available

The pre-application will include project information, total generation capacity and total generation limit, peak load of feeders/lines and substations. The pre-application report produced by ComEd is non-binding and does not confer any rights. In addition, the Interconnection Customer must submit an interconnection application to receive approval to interconnect with the ComEd system.

Once submitted, the application will undergo an initial screening to ensure the application is complete and all associated fees are paid in full, before proceeding to the Technical Review process.

## Technical Reviews

The technical review process for DER interconnection is performed pursuant to the appropriate review level set forth in the interconnection rules, and is primarily based on the size of the proposed DER and configuration of the local electric distribution facilities. [Appendix 1](13-appendix-1-process-timeline.md) illustrates the various levels of review and timelines.

Upon receipt of a complete application and payment, a review of each interconnection application is performed to identify any adverse system impacts that may result from interconnection of the proposed DER facility, and the corresponding interconnection facilities and distribution upgrades required to mitigate those impacts. The following elements may be included in the review:

- Service and Metering Requirements
- Telemetry Requirements
- Real Estate Requirements

### Feasibility Studies

- Initial identification of any circuit breaker short circuit capability limits exceeded because of the interconnection.
- Initial identification of any thermal overload, voltage limit violations, or other adverse system impacts resulting from the interconnection
- Description and non-binding estimated cost of interconnection facilities and distribution upgrades, itemized in line item format.

### System Impact Studies

- Initial review of grounding requirements, including review per IEEE C62.92.6 for inverterbased DER when additional grounding equipment is considered, and system protection.
- Description and non-binding estimated cost of interconnection facilities and distribution upgrades, itemized in line item format.

### Limited Export (including Limited Power Rating or Derated Systems) and Non-Exporting System

Certain methods of export control are allowed per 466.75 Limited-Export and Non-Exporting Distribution Energy Generation Resources Facilities. The Export Capacity specified by an Interconnection Customer will be included as a limitation in the interconnection agreement.

Proposed control or protection settings for a system designed to operate at less than nameplate capacity are required before an application can be evaluated.

The export controls identified in ICC Part 466.75 subsections (c)(1) through (3) are acceptable for non-exporting systems only. The export control identified in ICC Part 466.75 subsections (c)(4) and (c)(5) may be used for limited export systems only. The export controls in ICC Part 466.75 subsections (c)(6) and (7) may be used for either non-exporting or limited export systems. Inverter-based limited export systems shall use advanced inverters utilizing operating modes and settings specified by the ComEd.

Non-exporting systems utilizing protective relays should provide either Dev 32R (Reverse Power Protection) or Dev 32F (Minimum Import Protection) and shall utilize the protection settings prescribed in ICC Part 466.75 subsections (c)(1) or (c)(2) based on the protective function being implemented for non-exporting systems utilizing protective relays.

Any DER system utilizing a limited power rating or derated power rating less than the nameplate rating of the DER system shall comply with all of the applicable requirements contained in ICC Part 466.75 subsection (c)(5) Configured Power Rating. These requirements listed below include: providing alternative nameplate rating labels and a certified letter from the manufacturer confirming the reduced capacity.

A reduced power output rating that utilizes the power rating configuration setting may be used to ensure the DER does not generate power beyond a specific value lower than the nameplate capacity. The reduced power rating shall be indicated by means of a nameplate rating replacement, or by a supplemental adhesive nameplate rating tag to indicate the reduced nameplate rating. At the discretion of the ComEd, the Interconnection Customer may additionally be required to provide a letter from the manufacturer confirming the reduced capacity.

All limited export and non-exporting systems covered under ICC Part 466.75, excluding subsection (c)(3), may be required, at the discretion of the ComEd, to be field verified. This may include verification of new equipment nameplate labels with new configured power ratings and/or performance of witness testing to verify compliance with either the limited export or nonexporting controls as required as part of the interconnection request and agreement prior to being provided with Permission to Operate.
