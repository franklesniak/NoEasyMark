# Design and Operating Requirements for Customer Equipment

## Interconnecting Customer Engineering Design

The customer is responsible for designing its own DER step-up electrical facility that corresponds to ComEd standards. The Interconnection Customer shall provide technical data for each device, such as engineering specifications, settings data, etc. for ComEd review. ComEd review of customer documents and settings is for the reliability and safety of the ComEd system ONLY and does not imply adequate protection or performance of customer-owned facilities. Refer to [Appendix 4: Relay and Protection Requirements](16-appendix-4-relay-protection.md), which provides general relay functional requirements and [Appendix 5](17-appendix-5-plan-a.md) provides typical relay diagrams for different types of DER. [Appendix 6: Examples of Generators and Step-Up Transformer Data Sheets](20-appendix-6-data-sheets.md), provides example lists of data that could be requested in specific cases, for generators and step-up transformers.

During the Technical Review ComEd's functional relay requirements specific to the proposed DER facility will be given to the DER Interconnection Customer, using the Relay Functional Requirements Specifications (RFRS) form. This form will be generated based on the initial information provided in the submitted interconnection application.

The RFRS form is project-specific and shows the protective functions for which the DER Interconnection Customer is to provide relays and related equipment. The Interconnection Customer must indicate on the RFRS form the specific relay type and range proposed for each function. The DER Interconnection Customer must also provide proposed current and potential transformer ratios, connections, and locations as related to the electrical one-line diagram. The completed RFRS forms and related information, including the corresponding one- line diagrams, are to be returned to ComEd.

ComEd cannot proceed with design until the demarcation drawing is complete and approved by the customer and ComEd (see the Real Estate section of this document). In addition, ComEd drawings will not be issued for construction until applicable Deed(s) and/or Title Commitments are also provided by the customer.

The Interconnection Customer must receive ComEd approval of Customer design documents before proceeding with construction. In addition, ComEd will not proceed with construction activities until the Grant of Easement has been executed by the applicable landowners and recorded with the proper governing entities (i.e. County, etc.). Project delays due to untimely submittal of complete design documents, demarcation drawing, ALTA CAD files, or Grant of Easement approvals are the responsibility of the Interconnection Customer.

Design documents must be of good engineering quality and shall be stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.E.) Registered in the State of Illinois as required by the Illinois Professional Engineering Act (225 ILCS 325/) Professional Engineering Practice Act of 1989. ComEd however does NOT require Level 1 or Level 2 applications less than <1.0MW be stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.E.) Registered in the State of Illinois unless specifically required by the Illinois Professional Engineering Act. Design engineering documents and legal documents including the following shall be submitted at the time of application:

- One-line diagram showing the connections between the generator(s) and the ComEd System\*
- Three-line diagrams showing current and potential circuits for protective relays\*
- Relay tripping and control schematic diagram\*
- Instruction books for relays on the RFRS forms
- Protective Relay Settings\*
- Site plan with POI indicated, access road location (if applicable), and array layout
- Equipment data sheets
- When an interconnection customer is not currently a customer of the ComEd at the proposed site, the interconnection customer shall provide, at the time of the interconnection request, Proof of the interconnection customer's legal right to control the site via:
  - Evidenced by the interconnection customer's name on a property tax bill
  - Deed
  - Lease agreement
  - Option agreement, or
  - Other legally binding contract

*\*Denotes PM stamp is required on document per the preceding paragraph.*

Additional engineering meetings may be necessary to discuss the design documents. If changes are necessary, the Interconnection Customer must incorporate all changes and corrections and submit corrected prints to ComEd before proceeding with construction.

## External Disconnect Requirements

Section ICC Part 466.60 states, in fact: "To protect the safety of the Electric Distribution Company [(ComEd)'s] employees or the reliability of the distribution system, [ComEd] may require that distributed generation facilities have the capability to be isolated from [ComEd]:

- For distributed generation facilities interconnecting to a primary line, the isolation shall be by means of a lockable, visible-break isolation device accessible by [ComEd].
- For distributed generation facilities interconnecting to a secondary line, the isolation shall be by means of a lockable isolation device whose status is indicated and is accessible by [ComEd].
- For distributed generation facilities interconnecting to a secondary line through a self-contained meter, [ComEd's] removal of the self-contained electric meter may satisfy this capability.

The isolation device shall be installed, owned and maintained by the owner of the distributed generation facility and located electrically between the distributed generation facility and the point of interconnection. A draw-out type of circuit breaker accessible to [ComEd] with a provision for padlocking at the drawn-out position satisfies the requirement for an isolation device."

The interconnection customer shall isolate the distributed generation facility when requested by ComEd's authorized representative. ICC Part 466.60 states, in fact: "An interconnection customer may elect to provide [ComEd] with access to an isolation device that is contained in a building or area that may be unoccupied and locked or not otherwise accessible to [ComEd] by installing a lockbox provided by [ComEd] that allows ready access to the isolation device. The lockbox shall be in a location determined by [ComEd] to be accessible by [ComEd]. The interconnection customer shall permit [ComEd] to affix a placard in a location of its choosing that provides instructions to [ComEd] operating personnel for accessing the isolation device. If [ComEd] needs to isolate the distributed generation facility, [ComEd] shall not be held liable for any damages resulting from the actions necessary to isolate the generation facility."

- ComEd requires an external disconnect switch on all Distributed Energy Resources (DER) equipped services, except for single-phase self-contained 120V and 120/240V services rated 200 amperes or below. The customer shall furnish, install, own, and maintain this switch.
- Customer shall install and maintain a visual-open, manually operated, load break disconnect switch ("Disconnect Switch") that will completely open and isolate all ungrounded conductors of Customer's generating facility from the ComEd System. For multi-phase systems, the switch shall be gang-operated.
- The external disconnect shall have a short-circuit current rating equal to or greater than the available fault current.
- The external disconnect switch shall isolate all on-site DER from the ComEd system.
- The switch shall be located within line of sight of the ComEd revenue meter, but not more than 20 feet away, and shall be continuously accessible to authorized ComEd representatives.
- If a switch within sight of the meters is not practical, the switch may be in another continuously accessible location if a prominent sign is located at the meters indicating the location of the switch.
- The switch shall be lockable in the open position. It shall be possible to see the switch contacts and verify they are open by opening the switch cover (as in a safety switch), by using a viewing window, or by comparable means.
- For primary disconnect enclosures, 8-foot minimum (measured from the edge of the pad) in front of all equipment doors to provide room to operate with hot sticks and to replace the equipment.
- For secondary service, 3-foot minimum clearance in front of the switch to provide room for safe operation.
