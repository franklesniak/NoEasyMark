# Real Estate and Equipment Maintenance

## Real Estate

It is the Interconnection Customer's responsibility to purchase property, acquire rights and obtain any required permits for interconnecting its generation station to the point of demarcation. The Interconnection Agreement stipulates that the Interconnection Customer will obtain, purchase, or grant such rights and interests as may be reasonably necessary to interconnect the generation station to the ComEd System. This includes all rights, including, but not limited to, any parcel where ComEd work takes place to support the interconnection upgrades. It is ComEd's practice to provide drafted easements, where required, for all impacted parcels and it will be the Interconnection Customer's responsibility to obtain signatures from any landowners.

Community Supply Customers, and some Commercial & Industrial Customers, are required to provide ALTA Computer Aided Design (CAD) files to ComEd to finalize demarcation drawings. Approved demarcation drawings and Vesting Deed(s) and/or Title Commitment(s) shall be provided to ComEd to secure any Grant of Easement approvals from ComEd.

## Customer Maintenance of Equipment

The Interconnection Customer must provide ComEd with calibration and functional test data for the associated equipment upon request. Minimum intervals are indicated below:

| Equipment | Calibration and Functional Test Minimum Interval |
| --- | --- |
| Relays | Every three years |
| Communication Channels | Every three years |
| Circuit breakers | Every three years |
| Batteries | Per IEEE 450 - 1995 Standard |

The customer must include the identities and qualifications of the personnel who performed the tests. ComEd personnel may need to periodically witness the testing.
